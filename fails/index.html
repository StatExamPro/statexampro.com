STAT FAILS #1

The counsellors who were 100% sure

In 1998 a researcher posing as a low-risk client asked 20 German AIDS counselling centres whether a positive screening test could be a false alarm. Most said no, several quoted 100% certainty; the honest answer was about 50%. Base-rate neglect, documented on the professionals who ran the test every day.

STAT FAILS #2

The pill scare that caused 13,000 abortions

In 1995 UK doctors were warned that third-generation pills doubled the risk of a blood clot. The absolute increase was one clot per 7,000 women, smaller than the clot risk of the pregnancies that followed. The scare is estimated to have caused about 13,000 extra abortions in England and Wales.

STAT FAILS #3

The sepsis AI that missed two thirds of the sepsis

A sepsis model built into Epic ran in hundreds of US hospitals, sold with an AUC of 0.76 to 0.83. An independent validation on 38,000 hospitalizations measured 0.63: two thirds of sepsis cases missed, alerts fired on 18% of all patients, most of them false.
